Normal Subgroup

Motivation & Definition

Fix a group $G$ and consider the category $\cat{B}G$ defined$^\dagger$ by $G$. What are the congruences in $\cat{B}G$? In this category, a congruence is an equivalence relation $\sim$ on $G$ that respects the group operation, that is, $g_1\sim g_2$ and $h_1\sim h_2$ implies $g_1h_1\sim g_2h_2$.


  It turns out that these congruences correspond directly to special subgroups of $G$, namely, the normal ones:

Definition.

A subgroup $N\substructeq G$ is said to be normal if it is invariant under conjugation by $G$; i.e., if $gN=Ng$ for all $g\in G$.


  In this case, we write $N\nsubgrpeq G$, and $N\nsubgrp G$ if it is proper.

Equivalently, we may demand $gng^{-1}\in N$ for all $n\in N$ and $g\in G$ instead.

Normal $\Leftrightarrow$ Congruence

We show that every congruence relation $\sim$ on $G$ is induced by a unique normal subgroup $N\nsubgrpeq G$.

Proof. Click to expand...

Given a congruence $\sim$, the subset $N\coloneqq\l\{n\in G\st n\sim e\r\}$ is clearly a subgroup, and is normal since for all $gn\in gN$, we have $gn\sim g$ and hence $gng^{-1}\sim e$. Thus $gng^{-1}\in N$.

  Conversely, given a normal subgroup $N\nsubgrpeq G$, the relation $\sim$ on $G$ defined by $g_1\sim g_2$ iff $g_2^{-1}g_1\in N$ is clearly an equivalence, and is a congruence since if $g_1\sim g_2$ and $h_1\sim h_2$, then $$\begin{equation} (g_2h_2)^{-1}(g_1h_1)=h_2^{-1}(g_2^{-1}g_1)h_1\in h_2^{-1}Nh_1=h_2^{-1}h_1N=N.\qedin \end{equation}$$

This equivalence allows us to take quotients $G/N$ of $G$ by normal subgroups $N\nsubgrpeq G$, and in particular makes $\pi:G\onto G/N$ a group homomorphism. Such a construction allows for another equivalence; namely, that normal subgroups correspond to kernels:

Proof. Click to expand... If $N\nsubgrpeq G$ is a normal subgroup, then it is the kernel of $\pi:G\onto G/N$. Conversely, for any homomorphism $f:G\to H$, its kernel $\ker f$ is normal since if $f(h)=0$ and $g\in G$, then $f(ghg^{-1})=f(g)f(h)f(g)^{-1}=0$ too.$\blacksquare$

Normal Closure

Each equivalence relation $\sim$ induces a congruence (obtained by intersecting every congruence containing $\sim$). In particular, for a subgroup $H\substructeq G$, the congruence relation induced by the equivalence relation $g_1\sim g_2$ iff $g_1H=g_2H$ corresponds by the above to a normal subgroup $\gen{H}_N\nsubgrpeq G$, called the normal closure of $H$. Equivalently, $\gen{H}_N$ is the smallest normal subgroup of $G$ containing $H$, obtained by either closing under conjugation or by taking the intersection of all normal subgroups containing $H$.
