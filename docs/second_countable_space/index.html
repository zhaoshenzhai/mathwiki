Second Countable Space

Motivation & Definition

One way to measure the ‘complexity’ of a topological space is to limit the cardinality of bases thereof, and since the most important cardinal is probably $\aleph_0$, we make the following

Definition. A topological space $X$ is second countable if it admits a countable basis.

In the setting of a metrizable space, this limitation is equivalent to some others (like, separability, being Lindelöf, and the CCC). In general, however, second countability is stronger than the rest.
