Quotient Group

Motivation & Definition

Fix an equivalence relation on a group $G$ and consider the quotient set $G/\!\sim$. Since $\sim$ has, a priori, nothing to do with the group structure of $G$, we cannot expect $G/\!\sim$ to carry a natural group structure.


  Instead, we need to consider a congruence $\sim$ on $G$, which (in the category $\catgrp$) is an equivalence relation $\sim$ such that $g_1\sim g_2$ and $h_1\sim h_2$ implies $g_1h_1\sim g_2h_2$. This suffices to induce a group structure on $G/\!\sim$, whose identity is the normal subgroup $N\nsubgrpeq G$ induced $^\dagger$ by $\sim$ and whose operation is $[g]\ast[h]\coloneqq[gh]$.

Cosets/Normal Subgroups

Since the normal subgroup induced by $\sim$ is given by $N=\l\{n\in G\st n\sim e\r\}$, we have $[g]=\l\{gn\in G\st n\in N\r\}\eqqcolon gN$ for every $g\in G$, called the (left) coset of $N$ by $g$. Thus, the standard definition is the following.

Definition. Let $N\nsubgrpeq G$ be a normal subgroup. The quotient group of $G$ by $N$ is the group $G/N\coloneqq G/\!\sim$ equipped with the operation $gN\ast hN\coloneqq(gh)N$.

If $g_1\sim g_2$ and $h_1\sim h_2$, then $g_1h_1\sim g_2h_2$.$\blacksquare$

Remark. In this form, the canonical projection $\pi:G\onto G/N$ sends $g\mapsto gN$. Almost by definition, we have that $\ker\pi=N$.$\blacklozenge$

Fibers of Homomorphisms

Given the above construction, and recalling that normal subgroups correspond $^\dagger$ to kernels, we may a posteriori define the quotient group of $G$ w.r.t. a homomorphism $f:G\to H$ as the set of fibers of $f$ equipped with the operation $f^{-1}(h_1)\ast f^{-1}(h_2)\coloneqq f^{-1}(h_1h_2)$. Its identity is, of course, $\ker f$.


  Every such ‘fiber group’ arise as fibers of $\pi:G\onto G/N$. Conversely, they induce the quotient group $G/\ker f$.

Categorical Aspects

Universal Property

Quotient groups satisfy the following universal property:

Proposition. Let $N\nsubgrpeq G$ be a normal subgroup. The projection $\pi:G\onto G/N$ is initial amongst all homomorphisms $f:G\to H$ such that $N\subseteq\ker f$.
Proof.

That $N\subseteq\ker f$ is equivalent to $$\begin{equation} \fa g_1,g_2\in G:g_1\sim g_2\Longrightarrow f(g_1)=f(g_2), \end{equation}$$ for which the universal property

$^\dagger$ of the

quotient set $G/\!\sim$ furnishes a unique function $f’:G/N\to H$ such that $f=f’\circ\pi$. This is a homomorphism since $$\begin{equation} f’([g_1]\ast[g_2])=f’([g_1g_2])=f’(\pi(g_1g_2))=f(g_1g_2)=f(g_1)f(g_2)=f’([g_1])f’([g_2]), \end{equation}$$ as desired.$\blacksquare$

Quotient Category

Viewing $G$ as its corresponding category $\cat{B}G$ (consisting of a single object $\ast$, and morphisms $\Hom(\ast,\ast)=G$), the quotient category $\cat{B}G/\!\sim$ by a congruence $\sim$ is precisely (the category corresponding to) the quotient $G/N$.
